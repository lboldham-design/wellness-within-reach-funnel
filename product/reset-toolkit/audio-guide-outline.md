# Audio Guide Outline & Narration Scripts
## The 5-Minute Nervous System Reset Toolkit
**Wellness Within Reach**

---

*These scripts are written for self-recording or professional narration. Recommended recording: calm, unhurried pace with 1–2 second pauses at each line break. Background: soft ambient sound (rain, white noise, or silence). No music needed.*

---

## Session 1: Morning Wake-Up
**Duration: 5 minutes | Best time: Before reaching for your phone**

---

*[Pause 2 seconds before speaking]*

Good morning.

Take a breath in through your nose... and let it go slowly through your mouth.

You don't need to do anything else yet. Just arrive.

*[4-second pause]*

Let your eyes open softly — not searching, just looking. Notice three things in the room around you. Let your gaze rest on each one for a moment. Just noticing.

*[8-second pause]*

Now gently shake your hands out — loose wrists, soft fingers. Let your shoulders roll backward, slowly. Three times.

*[10-second pause]*

Nod your head gently — yes, then no. Let your neck soften.

*[8-second pause]*

Now we'll breathe together.

Inhale through your nose — 1, 2, 3, 4.
Hold — 1, 2.
Exhale slowly through your mouth — 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6.

*[3-second pause]*

Again. Inhale — 1, 2, 3, 4.
Hold — 1, 2.
Exhale — 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6.

*[3-second pause]*

One more time. Let this exhale be a little longer — like setting something down.

*[4-second pause]*

Now bring your attention to your body — starting at the top of your head. Move slowly downward. Is there any place that feels tight or held?

Notice it without trying to fix it. Just breathe into it on the inhale — and let the exhale soften it a little.

*[15-second pause]*

Before you stand, take a moment to set an intention.

What quality do you want to bring to today?
It might be patience. Presence. Ease. Steadiness. Curiosity.

Choose one. Feel it. Let it be a quiet intention — not a pressure, just a direction.

*[8-second pause]*

Take one last full breath in...

...and let it go.

You're ready for the day. Go gently.

*[END]*

## Session 2: Midday Reset
**Duration: 5 minutes | Best time: 2–3pm or after a difficult interaction**

---

*[Pause 2 seconds]*

Take a moment to step away from whatever you're doing.

Just for five minutes, you're allowed to stop.

*[4-second pause]*

If possible, run cold water over the inside of your wrists right now. Let it run for 30 seconds. Feel the temperature. Let it interrupt the cycle.

*[35-second pause — cold water time]*

Good.

Now stand up — even if just for a moment. Reach both arms overhead. Interlace your fingers if you can. Feel the stretch through your sides.

*[10-second pause]*

Shake your arms out. Shake your hands. Roll your ankles slowly — one way, then the other.

*[10-second pause]*

Let's do box breathing together.

Inhale — 1, 2, 3, 4.
Hold — 1, 2, 3, 4.
Exhale — 1, 2, 3, 4.
Hold — 1, 2, 3, 4.

*[3-second pause]*

Again. In — 1, 2, 3, 4.
Hold — 1, 2, 3, 4.
Out — 1, 2, 3, 4.
Hold — 1, 2, 3, 4.

*[3-second pause]*

Two more rounds at your own pace. Keep the count steady.

*[30-second pause]*

Now do something slowly that you usually rush: drink a glass of water. Take a few bites of something. Step outside for a breath.

Give it your full attention. Just for one minute. Not as a task — as medicine.

*[60-second pause]*

You've reset.

You can go back now — a little clearer, a little more present.

*[END]*

## Session 3: Emotional Spike
**Duration: 5 minutes | Best time: When you feel anger, panic, or overwhelm rising**

---

*[Calm, unhurried pace — slightly slower than other sessions]*

Whatever just happened — you can set it down for five minutes.

You don't have to fix it right now. You don't have to figure it out.
Just — be here.

*[4-second pause]*

Start by naming what you're feeling.

You don't have to say it out loud. Just notice it. Anger. Anxiety. Hurt. Fear. Shame. Exhaustion.

Name it internally: "I am feeling ______."

*[6-second pause]*

Now — a double inhale.

Breathe in through your nose — then take one more small sniff at the top.
Then release it all slowly through your mouth.

*[5-second pause]*

Again. Double inhale — sniff at the top — long exhale.

*[5-second pause]*

One more time.

*[5-second pause]*

Now move your body — even just for a moment. Walk around the room. Shake your arms. Roll your shoulders. Do five jumping jacks if you can.

The emotion is energy. Let it move through you.

*[30-second pause]*

Now sit down. Both feet flat on the floor. Both palms on your thighs.

Feel the pressure. Feel the weight of your body in the seat.

*[6-second pause]*

Five slow belly breaths. Let each exhale be audible — like a real sigh, not a polite one.

*[40-second pause]*

Now — soften one thing.

Your jaw. Your shoulders. The skin around your eyes.

Let it go just a little.

*[8-second pause]*

You don't have to be okay right now. You just have to be here.

And you are.

*[END]*

## Session 4: Pre-Sleep Wind-Down
**Duration: 7 minutes | Best time: 30 minutes before sleep**

---

*[Slower, quieter, warmer tone throughout]*

The day is done.

Whatever didn't get finished... it will wait.

Right now — this moment, this breath — is where you are.

*[5-second pause]*

Make sure the room is dim. The temperature is cool if you can manage it. Your phone is face-down or away.

You don't need to be anywhere but here.

*[8-second pause]*

We're going to move through your body from feet to face. For each area, I'll ask you to tense the muscles gently, then let them go completely.

Starting with your feet and calves — squeeze them gently... hold... 2, 3, 4, 5...

And release. Feel them soften into the bed.

*[6-second pause]*

Your thighs — squeeze... hold... 2, 3, 4, 5...

And release.

*[6-second pause]*

Your belly — pull it gently inward... hold... 2, 3, 4, 5...

And let go. Let your breath breathe itself.

*[8-second pause]*

Your hands — make soft fists... squeeze... and then spread your fingers wide, wide, wide...

And release. Let your hands rest open.

*[6-second pause]*

Your shoulders — shrug them up toward your ears... hold... 2, 3, 4, 5...

And drop. Feel the weight of your arms.

*[8-second pause]*

Your jaw — clench gently... hold... 2, 3...

And let it release. Let your mouth hang slightly open. Let your tongue rest.

*[8-second pause]*

Your whole body is heavier now. Softer.

*[5-second pause]*

Let's breathe together — this time, the exhale is twice as long as the inhale.

In — 1, 2, 3, 4.
Out — 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8.

*[3-second pause]*

In — 1, 2, 3, 4.
Out — 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8.

*[3-second pause]*

Continue at your own pace. Let the exhales get longer if they want to.

*[30-second pause]*

As you breathe... you don't need to solve anything tonight.

You don't need to figure anything out.

You've done enough for today.

You can rest now.

*[Fade — END]*

## Session 5: ADHD Freeze
**Duration: 5 minutes | Best time: When your brain hits the wall**

---

*[Slightly more energetic — grounding but activating]*

You've hit the wall. I know that place.

Here's the thing: this is not a character flaw. This is not laziness.
This is your nervous system in freeze. It's neurological. And it responds to specific things.

Let's do those things.

*[3-second pause]*

First — say it out loud, even quietly:
"I am in freeze. This is my nervous system. It will pass."

*[5-second pause]*

Now — move.

Stand up right now and walk somewhere. Even just across the room and back.

If you can — do 10 jumping jacks.
If not — shake your hands vigorously for 30 seconds.
Or stand and march in place for one minute.

Whatever you choose — do it now. I'll be here when you're back.

*[90-second pause]*

Okay. You moved. That matters.

Now reduce what's around you. Close browser tabs. Silence your phone. If the environment is loud — headphones on, brown noise or lo-fi. Lower the noise so your prefrontal cortex can come back online.

*[20-second pause]*

Now ask yourself one question:

*What is the smallest possible next action — something I could do in the next 2 minutes?*

Not the project. Not the list.
One tiny thing.

Open the file. Write one sentence. Reply to one email.

*[8-second pause]*

Go do it. Right now. Before you think too hard about it.

*[pause]*

And when you've done it — notice that. Notice you moved from frozen to done.

That is not nothing. That is everything.

*[END]*

## Session 6: Coming Home to Yourself
**Duration: 10 minutes | For disconnection, depletion, or when you've lost touch with yourself**

---

*[Warmest, most personal tone of all sessions]*

This one is for when you've been so busy taking care of everything and everyone that you can't quite feel yourself anymore.

When the day is full but you feel empty.
When you're going through the motions but you're not quite there.

This is for you.

*[5-second pause]*

Start by finding a comfortable position — seated or lying down. Let your hands rest somewhere they feel supported.

Close your eyes if that's comfortable.

*[6-second pause]*

Take three deep breaths — not performing them, just letting them happen. Let your body breathe the way it wants to.

*[20-second pause]*

Now bring your attention to your chest. Not your heart as an organ — just the area of your chest.

Notice what's there.
It might be tension. Sadness. Emptiness. Warmth. Grief. Longing. Relief.
Whatever is there — let it be there. Just observe.

*[15-second pause]*

Place one hand gently over that area.

Feel the warmth of your own hand.

*[8-second pause]*

Say quietly, to yourself:

*I've been working so hard.*

Let that land.

*[8-second pause]*

*I've been carrying a lot.*

*[8-second pause]*

*I am allowed to come home to myself now.*

*[10-second pause]*

Let yourself breathe into that. As though you're slowly returning to a place that's been waiting for you.

*[15-second pause]*

Think of one thing — just one — that you genuinely enjoy. That has nothing to do with productivity or being useful.

A place. A feeling. A smell. A moment in time. A person's laugh.

Let yourself go there for a moment. Just visit it in your mind.

*[20-second pause]*

Notice — even the thought of it changes your body slightly.

That's real. That matters. Pleasure is regulatory.

*[8-second pause]*

As you prepare to return to your day — take this with you:

You are not just a function.
You are not just what you do for others.
You are a person with a nervous system, a history, and a need for care.

And you are allowed to receive that care — from yourself, first.

*[10-second pause]*

Take one last breath in — fully, all the way.

And let it go — completely, all the way.

*[5-second pause]*

Welcome back.

*[END]*

---

*Recording Notes:*
- *Pace: approximately 110–120 words per minute for sessions 1–5, 95–100 for session 6*
- *Background: light ambient sound (rain, brown noise, or silence — no music)*
- *Tone: warm, unhurried, like a trusted friend — not clinical*
- *Equipment: any decent USB condenser microphone; free recording in Audacity or GarageBand*
- *Export as MP3, 128kbps or higher*
