## Card 1: Morning Wake-Up Reset

**5 minutes | Before your phone**

1. **Orient** — Look slowly around the room. Name 3 things you see. (60 sec)
2. **Shake & Wake** — Shake hands, roll shoulders back 3x, gentle head nods. (60 sec)
3. **Belly Breathe** — In 4, hold 2, out 6. Repeat 4 times. (90 sec)
4. **Body Scan** — Head to toe, breathe into any tension. (60 sec)
5. **Intention** — "Today I am choosing ______." (30 sec)

## Card 2: Midday Crash Reset

**5 minutes | 2–3pm slump or post-meeting fog**

1. **Cold Water** — Inside of wrists, 30 sec. Back of neck, brief. (60 sec)
2. **Stand & Stretch** — Arms overhead, interlace fingers. Shake out. (60 sec)
3. **Box Breathing** — In 4, hold 4, out 4, hold 4. 4–5 rounds. (2 min)
4. **Sensory Reset** — Drink water slowly. Feel air. One piece of fruit. (60 sec)

*You are not behind. You are recovering.*

## Card 3: Emotional Spike Reset

**5 minutes | Before it escalates**

1. **Name It** — "I am feeling ______. This is a nervous system response." (30 sec)
2. **Physiological Sigh** — Double inhale through nose. Long exhale. 3–4x. (60 sec)
3. **Movement Discharge** — Walk, jumping jacks, shake body. (90 sec)
4. **Ground** — Feet flat, palms on thighs. 5 slow belly breaths. (90 sec)
5. **Soften** — Unclench jaw. Drop shoulders. Soften eyes. (30 sec)

## Card 4: Pre-Sleep Wind-Down

**7 minutes | 30 min before bed**

1. **Environment** — Dim lights. Phone away. Cool the room. (2 min)
2. **Progressive Relaxation** — Feet → calves → thighs → belly → fists → shoulders → jaw. Squeeze 5, release. (3 min)
3. **Extended Exhale** — In 4, out 8. Repeat until you drift. (2 min)

*Sleep is regulation. Protect it.*

## Card 5: ADHD Freeze Reset

**5 minutes | When your brain hits the wall**

1. **Name It** — "I'm in freeze. Neurological, not laziness." (30 sec)
2. **Move** — Walk, jump, shake, dance. Anything. (2 min)
3. **Reduce** — Close tabs. Silence phone. Headphones on. (60 sec)
4. **One Tiny Thing** — Open the file. Write one sentence. Just one. (start now)
5. **Credit** — You moved from freeze. That counts.

## Card 6: Post-Conflict Recovery Reset

**5 minutes | After a hard conversation**

1. **Discharge** — Alone: cry, sound, say what you needed to say. (60 sec)
2. **Physiological Sigh** — Double inhale, long exhale. 3x. (30 sec)
3. **Cold Water** — Face or wrists. Interrupt the loop. (60 sec)
4. **Compassion Breath** — In: "I'm doing my best." Out: "So are they." (2 min)
5. **Anchor** — Name 3 stable things in your life right now. (60 sec)

## Card 7: Box Breathing

**2–4 minutes | Acute stress, before presentations, meetings, hard calls**

```
    INHALE 4
    ↗         ↘
HOLD 4       HOLD 4
    ↘         ↗
    EXHALE 4
```

Count slowly. Use your fingers if helpful.
Do 4–8 rounds. Eyes open or closed.

Used by Navy SEALs before high-stakes operations.

## Card 8: 4-7-8 Breathing

**2 minutes | Anxiety, racing thoughts, panic**

**Inhale through nose:** 1-2-3-4
**Hold:** 1-2-3-4-5-6-7
**Exhale through mouth:** 1-2-3-4-5-6-7-8

Repeat 4 times.

The extended hold increases oxygen exchange.
The long exhale activates the vagal brake.

*This will slow your heart rate. Let it.*

## Card 9: The Physiological Sigh

**30 seconds | Fastest-acting nervous system reset known**

1. Take a normal inhale through your nose.
2. At the top, take one more quick sniff in.
3. Exhale completely through your mouth — long and slow.

Repeat 3–4 times.

This collapses the air sacs in your lungs that balloon under stress and rapidly lowers CO2.

*You can do this at your desk. No one will notice.*

*Sage green · Wellness Within Reach*

## Card 10: 5-4-3-2-1 Grounding

**2 minutes | Anxiety spirals, dissociation, panic**

**5** things you can **see** (name them)
**4** things you can **feel** (texture, temperature, weight)
**3** things you can **hear** (background, body, environment)
**2** things you can **smell** (present or memory)
**1** thing you can **taste**

Do this slowly. The slower, the more effective.

*ADHD tip: Walk while you do this. Name things out loud.*

*Sage green · Wellness Within Reach*

## Card 11: Cold Water Reset

**30–60 seconds | Heart racing, about to cry, post-adrenaline crash**

**Option A:** Run cold water over the inside of your wrists for 30 seconds.

**Option B:** Splash cold water on your face.

**Option C:** Hold an ice cube in your palm until it melts.

This activates the mammalian dive reflex — a hard-wired response that slows your heart rate by 10–25% within seconds.

*No willpower required. Just cold water.*

*Sage green · Wellness Within Reach*

## Card 12: The One Tiny Thing

**ADHD Freeze Breaker | When starting feels impossible**

**Don't ask:** *What do I need to do?*

**Ask instead:** *What is the smallest possible next action — something I can do in 2 minutes?*

Open the document.
Write one sentence.
Reply to one email.
Make one phone call.
Wash three dishes.

Dopamine releases on completion — however small.
One tiny thing creates the momentum for the next.

**You are not lazy. You are dopamine-depleted.**

*Feed the system. Start tiny.*
